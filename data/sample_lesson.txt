Photosynthesis is the process plants use to make their own food. Plants need sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water. The leaves take in sunlight and carbon dioxide. The roots absorb water from the soil. The plant uses these ingredients to make sugar, which gives the plant energy. Oxygen is released into the air as a byproduct.

This process is important because plants are producers. Producers make their own food and provide energy for other living things. Animals and humans also depend on the oxygen that plants release.
